## What is the AACC?
The AACC provides a simple, three-category system for attributing AI use in creative work across writing, visual art, music, film, and more.

## The Three Categories

### 1. HUMAN AUTHORED
- The work is conceived, developed, and constructed entirely by the creator
- AI does not contribute to the creative content
- Incidental AI use (grammar tools, spell-check, research) does not disqualify this category
- Attribution: `By [Creator Name]`

**Examples:**
- Writing: "Mercury." By Sarah Chen
- Film: *Venus.* Dir. by Chris Taylor
- Music: "Mars." By Luna Rivers
- Visual Art: *Jupiter.* By Jamila Banks

### 2. AI-ASSISTED
- The creator originates and drives the work
- AI contributes through modification, enhancement, or refinement at any stage
- The creator makes all major creative decisions and is responsible for the final work
- If AI produces the initial draft or primary material, classify the work as AI-Generated instead
- Attribution: `By [Creator Name], AI-Assisted by [AI Name]`

**Examples:**
- Writing: "Mercury." By Sarah Chen, AI-Assisted by Apertus (line-level editing and word choice)
- Film: *Venus.* Dir. by Chris Taylor, AI-Assisted by Runway ML (color grading and visual effects enhancement)
- Music: "Mars." By Luna Rivers, AI-Assisted by Wondera (harmonic progressions and arrangement refinement)
- Visual Art: *Jupiter.* By Jamila Banks, AI-Assisted by Midjourney (texture enhancement and composition refinement)

### 3. AI-GENERATED
- AI generates the primary content from the creator's prompts and direction
- The creator's role is conceptual, curatorial, and editorial
- Attribution: `AI-Generated by [AI Name], concept by [Creator Name]`

**Examples:**
- Writing: "Saturn." AI-Generated by Apertus, concept by Jordan Lee (Lee provided plot outline and characters; AI wrote primary draft; Lee edited for voice and pacing)
- Film: *Uranus.* AI-Generated by Runway ML, concept by Sam Rivera (Rivera provided scene descriptions; AI generated video sequences; Rivera edited and color graded)
- Music: "Neptune." AI-Generated by Wondera, concept by Sofia Razo (Razo specified genre and mood; AI composed and produced track; Razo added transitions and mastered)
- Visual Art: *Pluto Demoted.* AI-Generated by Midjourney, concept by Kai Thompson (Thompson crafted prompts; AI generated imagery; Thompson selected and refined final work)

## Why the AACC?

- **Simple:** Three clear categories, easy to understand and apply
- **Universal:** Works across all creative disciplines
- **Practical:** Designed for real-world attribution (publications, film descriptions, song credits, artist statements)
- **Flexible:** Optional detail allows creators to specify exactly how AI was used
- **Open:** Free to use and adapt under [Creative Commons Attribution 4.0 (CC BY 4.0)](https://creativecommons.org/licenses/by/4.0/)

---

## How to Use
1. Review the three categories
2. Ask yourself: **What role did AI play in this work?**
   - If AI played no creative role → **Human Authored**
   - If you created and drove the work and AI helped refine or enhance it → **AI-Assisted**
   - If AI generated the primary content from your direction → **AI-Generated**
3. Use the attribution format for your category
4. Optionally add details in parentheses about what the AI did per your discipline

## Use Examples by Category
**Category 1 (Human Authored):**
- Writer drafts and revises without AI tools
- Filmmaker shoots and edits without AI tools
- Musician composes and records without AI assistance
- Visual artist conceives and executes work without AI tools
- Photographer shoots, selects, and edits without AI assistance

**Category 2 (AI-Assisted):**
- Writer uses AI for line-level editing and word choice
- Filmmaker uses AI for color grading and visual effects enhancement
- Musician uses AI for harmonic progressions and arrangement refinement
- Visual artist uses AI to enhance texture and composition
- Photographer uses AI to enhance lighting

**Category 3 (AI-Generated):**
- AI creates full image from text prompt
- AI composes song from style parameters
- AI generates video from scene descriptions
- AI writes story from plot outline

[Also see →](https://davemalone.net/aacc)

## Adding Optional Detail

As in the examples above, you can add specificity about AI's role in parentheses:

**Basic:** `By Jordan Lee, AI-Assisted by Claude`

**Detailed:** `By Jordan Lee, AI-Assisted by Claude (line-level editing and restructuring of third act)`

This helps audiences understand exactly how AI contributed while keeping the base attribution simple.
